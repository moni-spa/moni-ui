# Divider

Dividers are thin lines that group content in lists or other containers

![Inbox app using full-width dividers to separate messages.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/01-inbox-app-using-full-width-dividers-to-separate-messages-18a4fe9c.png)

Full-width dividers

## Usage

Dividers are one way to visually group components and create hierarchy. They can also be used to imply nested parent/child relationships. The divider can be used in two ways: 

1. Full width
2. Inset

![Inbox app using full-width dividers to separate messages.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/02-inbox-app-using-full-width-dividers-to-separate-messages-c8d15b42.png)

## Full-width dividers

Use full-width dividers to separate larger sections of unrelated content. They can be used directly on a surface or inside other components like cards or lists. Full-width dividers can also separate interactive areas from non-interactive areas. They are used to group visual elements together, and indicate when elements are related to each other from an interaction perspective. 

![Inbox messages visually separated by full-width dividers for improved readability.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/05-inbox-messages-visually-separated-by-full-width-dividers-for-6450be1a.png)

Use full-width divider lines to separate interactive and non-interactive areas of a container such as a card

## Inset dividers

Use inset dividers to separate related content within a section. Inset dividers are equally indented from both sides of the screen by default.

![2 different content sections separated by an inset divider.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/08-2-different-content-sections-separated-by-an-inset-divider-698b47db.png)![2 different content sections separated by an inset divider.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/09-2-different-content-sections-separated-by-an-inset-divider-7d2ee8f1.png)

Inset dividers separate related content, such as emails in a list. They should be used with anchoring elements such as icons or avatars, and align with the leading edge of the screen.

![3 types of related content separated by inset dividers on a mobile screen.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/10-3-types-of-related-content-separated-by-inset-dividers-on-a--819bc466.png)![3 types of related content separated by inset dividers on a mobile screen.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/11-3-types-of-related-content-separated-by-inset-dividers-on-a--7b8e7caa.png)

### Using dividers both ways on the same screen

If dividers are used both ways in a UI, they must reinforce the hierarchy of information within different sections.

1. To separate a different kind of content, use a full-width divider
2. To separate nested content items, use inset dividers

![Dividers used to show content hierarchy: full-width for sections, inset for related items.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/14-dividers-used-to-show-content-hierarchy-full-width-for-secti-360eb48b.png)![Dividers used to show content hierarchy: full-width for sections, inset for related items.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/15-dividers-used-to-show-content-hierarchy-full-width-for-secti-cdce04d7.png)

check Do

Use a combination of inset and full-width dividers to reflect the hierarchy of information

List items with repetitive formats may not require an inset divider, in which using only the margin between items is acceptable.

![List with repetitive item formats. Visual separation achieved through margins only.](../../assets/divider/guidelines/16-list-with-repetitive-item-formats-visual-separation-achieved-46b368a2.png)

Content may not require a divider line

## Vertical divider

A vertical divider can be used to arrange content on a larger screen, such as separating paragraph text from video or imagery media.
